Commercial Thesis

From bespoke missions to infrastructure-grade intelligence

A hybrid revenue model evolving through three stages — custom flights, recurring monitoring, and SaaS analytics

Involve employs a hybrid revenue model that evolves from bespoke missions to scalable recurring contracts. It serves both civilian and defence ("dual-use") markets with stratospheric sensing platforms and data services. The business is transitioning from one-off flights toward continuous monitoring contracts and SaaS analytics through the CLEAR platform — mirroring the industry-wide shift from "price-per-kilometre" to "insights-as-a-service."

Involve's ambition is to become an infrastructure-grade intelligence platform — structurally analogous to Palantir's data-fusion model but with direct control over sensors. Its vertically-integrated stack (hardware → data → AI workflows) creates high switching costs and a compounding competitive moat.

Civil Track
Asset-intensive industries — operational efficiency
Targets energy, utilities, infrastructure, insurance, and municipalities where geospatial intelligence drives measurable operational efficiency: lower inspection costs, faster incident response, better underwriting models, and proactive maintenance.
  • Entry: pilot projects and use-case demonstrations
  • Expand: multi-year monitoring contracts per site
  • Scale: CLEAR SaaS subscriptions per vertical workflow
Defence & Institutional Track
Sovereign European sensing partner — mission-critical ISR
Positions Involve as a European sovereign sensing partner providing persistent, mission-critical intelligence. Defence and government clients follow formal RFP/tender processes with 12–36+ month cycles — but commit to multi-year framework agreements.
  • Entry: co-funded R&D and technology demonstrators
  • Expand: competitive tenders and EU/NATO frameworks
  • Scale: strategic supplier status and CLEAR integration

Revenue Architecture

Layered revenue stack — increasing margin up the value chain

Each layer builds on the one below — moving from hardware operations toward high-margin recurring software revenue

Involve's revenue flows through four layered offerings that increase in scale and margin as customers deepen their engagement. The strategic goal is to shift revenue mix progressively toward the upper layers — particularly CLEAR SaaS, which carries industry-benchmark gross margins of 70–90% with minimal incremental cost per additional customer. Internal targets: ~90% gross margin on the data-product layer.

Civil GTM

Civil strategy — five target segments and the land-and-expand funnel

Entry through specific operational pain points, expansion through monitoring contracts, lock-in through CLEAR workflows

Segment 01
Energy & Utilities
Oil & gas pipelines, electrical grids, water and gas networks. Large, fixed assets with safety, environmental, and regulatory risk. Satellite imagery and AI/ML for vegetation management along transmission corridors is already an operational standard in the US DOE guidance. Annual monitoring spend per km is economically justified by the cost of a single outage or leak incident.
Entry use case: Stratorelay® corridor pass → pipeline leak screening or vegetation encroachment mapping
Segment 02
Infrastructure & Construction
Transport networks (roads, bridges, rail), telecom towers, mining operations. Assets needing monitoring for structural condition, compliance, and change detection. Rail sector maintenance benchmarks suggest even a 1–3% allocation to monitoring analytics yields a multi-billion opportunity at continental scale.
Entry use case: Rail corridor inspection flight → structural deformation or embankment change detection
Segment 03
Environmental & Climate
Agencies and NGOs tracking deforestation, crop health, wildfire risk, and urban expansion. Regulatory and ESG mandates (CSRD, TNFD) are creating mandatory reporting obligations that require verified geospatial evidence — not estimates. Persistent stratospheric coverage enables the time-series data required for compliant disclosure.
Entry use case: Multi-spectral survey → NDVI baseline for regulatory compliance reporting
Segment 04
Insurance & Finance
Property/casualty insurers, reinsurers, and banks seeking risk assessment (floods, fires, construction defaults) and claims validation. Insurers already use satellite flood analytics to underwrite and respond to natural disasters. Persistent coverage provides the near-real-time imagery needed to convert manual claims validation into automated alert-triggered workflows.
Entry use case: Post-event flood imagery purchase → subscribe to CLEAR flood/fire alert services
Segment 05
Municipalities & Smart Cities
Urban planners and civil protection departments managing land use, disaster response, waste monitoring, and urban heat. Digital twin infrastructure requires persistent updating with high-cadence aerial data. CLEAR's natural language interface lowers the technical barrier for non-GIS users within public administrations.
Entry use case: Urban land-use change detection → continuous smart city monitoring dashboard via CLEAR

Civil GTM Funnel — Pilot → Monitor → SaaS
STAGE 01 — PILOT
Use-case driven flight
Customer funds a tailored flight to solve a specific operational problem — vegetation encroachment, pipeline screening, event damage assessment. Low commitment, high-impact demonstration. Involve delivers a result that is directly comparable to existing inspection costs, making ROI immediately visible.
Success metric: demonstrated cost saving or decision improvement vs existing method
STAGE 02 — CONTRACT
Multi-year monitoring agreement
Pilot success converts to a 3–5 year monitoring contract with scheduled coverage and a fixed annual fee. Involve becomes an operational supplier — not a project vendor. Customer benefits from budget predictability; Involve benefits from recurring ARR and reduced cost-of-sales on renewals.
Key insight: clients prefer predictable subscription pricing over ad-hoc buys — EO industry-wide trend
STAGE 03 — SAAS
CLEAR platform integration
Monitoring contract customer adopts CLEAR dashboards, API feeds, and automated alerts — co-developed around their specific workflows. CLEAR becomes embedded in operations: integrated with SCADA, GIS, or ERP systems. High switching costs. Upsell into new use cases (e.g. an energy utility moving from vegetation monitoring to grid stability forecasting).
Margin impact: CLEAR layer raises overall contract value significantly with minimal incremental delivery cost
Example — Energy Utility
Vegetation → Annual contract → CLEAR dashboards
An electric utility commissions a Stratorelay® flight to map vegetation risk near transmission lines. Seeing the cost savings from proactive trimming (proven by high-res monitoring case studies), it moves to an annual monitoring contract covering its full grid corridor. Later, it adopts CLEAR dashboards integrating satellite alerts into its maintenance workflow — broadening Involve's role from imagery provider to operational intelligence layer.
Example — P&C Insurer
Event imagery → Flood alerts → CLEAR risk dashboards
An insurer backs an initial flood imagery analysis to calibrate underwriting models. Once convinced of accuracy — leveraging data comparable to ICEYE's flood analytics currently used in insurance — it subscribes to periodic inundation reports. CLEAR provides user-friendly risk dashboards, converting a one-off event response into ongoing SaaS revenue. Historical archive value grows over time, increasing switching cost.
Defence & Institutional GTM

Defence strategy — procurement paths and sovereign positioning

Multi-year formal procurement cycles with high ACV and strategic supplier evolution — patience required, stickiness exceptional

Defence and government clients follow formal RFP/tender processes. Sales cycles are typically 12–36+ months with extensive evaluation, security certification requirements, and budget cycles measured in parliamentary terms. This fits Involve's model: persistent sensing infrastructure is a long-term capital-like commitment, not a discretionary purchase. Critically, defence contracts, once awarded, are highly sticky — agencies prefer committing to long-term programmes rather than re-running tenders.

Procurement path
Description, timing and Involve's positioning
Co-funded R&D / DemonstratorsEntry point — shared risk
Involve enters through collaborative research projects — EU/NATO tech programmes, PESCO initiatives, ESA co-funding — or small technology demonstrators. These allow government to share development risk and evaluate capability before committing to operational procurement. Success here leads to inclusion in later bids and builds the relationships needed to navigate full RFP processes. The existing Italian MoD €2M contract is the template: a demonstrator-level engagement that signals intent for scale-up.
Competitive Tenders & EU FrameworksMulti-year contracts · structured process
Involve bids on EU-level or national procurement tenders for multi-year monitoring systems. EU defence procurement uses structured processes (open/restricted tenders, competitive dialogue for complex systems) designed for transparency. The EU's emerging defence simplification directive permits 10-year framework agreements — enabling extended contracts that provide genuine revenue stability. Once Involve demonstrates capability, it can secure a place on such frameworks as an approved strategic supplier, allowing governments to "call-off" specific contracts efficiently without re-running full tenders.
Sovereign PositioningEuropean data autonomy narrative
European governments emphasise sovereignty in strategic intelligence. Involve's status as a European company with in-country platforms and full data custody is a structural selling point — offering unencumbered data with no foreign IP or control issues. This aligns with programmes for European sovereign EO and mirrors Satellogic's model of providing dedicated national capacity. Highlighting support for national missions (border surveillance, disaster relief, critical infrastructure protection) is essential in every defence engagement.
Civil Government / InstitutionalEnvironmental agencies · DGs · UN bodies
Civil government customers (environmental agencies, disaster management, civil protection) also use formal procurement but are often more flexible — some use annual budgets, grants, or inter-agency agreements. Involve targets programmes around national infrastructure, environment, and security. It may partner with prime contractors or join consortia. Multilateral agencies (EU Directorates-General, UN bodies) may procure sustained EO services through framework contracts that do not require individual country approval cycles.
Strategic Supplier EvolutionLong-term programme partnership
Successfully fulfilling initial contracts raises Involve to "preferred supplier" status. A national defence force might first buy occasional flights (capability demonstration), then sign a long-term coverage contract, and eventually integrate CLEAR into their intelligence systems. This evolution parallels mature defence supplier models: spot contracts → R&D programme → multi-year framework → embedded system integration. At that stage, switching costs are equivalent to replacing a core intelligence infrastructure layer.
Civil vs Defence sales — key differences: Defence deals emphasise capability and sovereignty — governments pay for the ability to task or collect anywhere, anytime, and will fund idle platform capacity unlike most commercial clients. Contracts include stringent IP/data terms, security standards compliance (ISO 27001, export controls), and require navigation of ministers, procurement officers, and military end-users simultaneously. Civil B2B sales are oriented around demonstrated ROI with simpler contracting cycles.

Data Strategy

Data scaling — four phases toward a proprietary data moat

Each phase adds proprietary data that competitors cannot replicate — compounding the value of the CLEAR intelligence layer

Involve's data assets evolve in phases. The strategic goal is to build a proprietary, continuously-growing data moat that makes CLEAR progressively more accurate, makes customers increasingly dependent on historical continuity, and makes competitive displacement progressively harder over time.

Phase 1Now
Public & In-House Data. Initially, Involve utilises open-source satellite data (Copernicus Sentinel, Landsat) and meteorological feeds to bootstrap CLEAR analytics. Combined with data from early stratospheric flights, this provides basic insights and validates the pipeline architecture. Using public data is common for early-stage EO projects — the differentiation at this stage is in the analytics layer and the sensor platform, not the data itself.
Phase 2Series A window
Expanding Proprietary Fleet. As Involve scales Stratostats®, Stratorelay®, and eventually LoonHive®, it accumulates unique high-altitude imagery, radar, and atmospheric sensing with no open-access equivalent. This proprietary data — continuous, high-cadence coverage over specific targets — adds exclusive value. Each new flight generates fresh labelled data that competitors cannot easily replicate. Over time, the accumulating time-series archive becomes the primary data moat: historical coverage depth is not purchasable, it must be earned through sustained operations.
Phase 32027–2028
Strategic Data Acquisitions. Once the core sensor base is established, Involve may selectively acquire or license complementary data — mid-resolution imagery from CubeSat constellations, licensed AIS vessel tracking data, or industrial IoT feeds. These acquisitions are tactical gap-filling (broadening CLEAR's coverage without duplicating what the stratospheric platforms already provide), not broad mercenary acquisitions. The goal is to ensure clients have a complete single-pane view without needing additional vendors.
Phase 42028–2029+
Multidomain Integration. Interlinks spaceborne (satellite), aerial (HAPS + UAV), and ground-based (IoT sensors, radar) data streams into a unified intelligence fabric. Analogous to Planet + Quantum Systems' satellite–drone Tip & Cue integration. CLEAR then ingests multi-domain inputs and enables holistic situational awareness that no single-domain provider can match — detecting a new structure via satellite, dispatching HAPS for closer inspection, correlating with ground sensor data for confirmation.
Defensibility mechanics: High switching costs emerge when customers embed Involve's unique data products and analytics into daily operations (monitoring dashboards, automated alerts, historical change comparison). Moving to a competitor would require retraining models on different data, rebuilding pipelines, and losing historical archive context. Vertical integration ensures data continuity — no license lapses, no third-party dependency — and allows optimisation: Involve can task its own assets to exactly the areas clients care about.
CLEAR Platform

CLEAR — from raw data to repeatable intelligence revenue

CLEAR is not "magic AI" — it is a disciplined analytics layer that converts more data into more insight and higher contract value

The CLEAR platform is Involve's intelligence core and the primary long-term margin driver. It transforms raw sensor feeds into actionable insights, drives upsell on existing monitoring contracts, and enables cross-vertical expansion without linear cost increases. CLEAR moves Involve from selling endpoints (balloons or imagery) to selling outcomes (actionable intelligence on a schedule).

Revenue driver 01
Increasing ARPU through analytics upsell
Customers who paid for imagery can pay extra for AI-driven alerts, trend dashboards, and reporting. This raises average revenue per user/contract without requiring additional platform flights. Clients see CLEAR as value-added — automated flood alerts for insurers, change-detection dashboards for utilities — and are willing to pay for the "last mile" of insight that raw data alone cannot provide.
Revenue driver 02
Converting monitoring to intelligence subscriptions
CLEAR layers on continuous data flows to deliver intelligence products: anomaly alerts, trend analyses, predictive models. Instead of delivering raw pipeline images, CLEAR flags potential leak signatures or encroachment events automatically. This shifts the offering from "you ask and we provide images" to "we deliver answers on your schedule" — the transition from EO 2.0 to intelligence-as-a-service that drives the industry's margin expansion.
Revenue driver 03
Cross-vertical R&D reuse — expand without linear cost
CLEAR's modular design means algorithms developed for one vertical reuse across others. Urban change-detection code serves oil rig monitoring; vegetation stress analysis applies to both power utilities and precision agriculture. New customers receive proven tools quickly. This reuse of R&D across verticals accelerates expansion into adjacent markets without proportional engineering investment — the core mechanism of platform-model unit economics.
Revenue driver 04
Multi-source fusion — single-pane intelligence view
CLEAR fuses Involve's multi-domain data and external feeds (weather, AIS, public satellite) into a unified system. Clients gain a single intelligence view across their entire operational landscape. This integration mirrors how Palantir's platforms unify disparate data sources — but here focused on geospatial and temporal layers, with the key difference that Involve controls the primary data source rather than depending on third-party data.
Revenue driver 05
Structured workflows — mission templates
CLEAR codifies customers' decision processes into "mission templates" — a compliance monitoring workflow, a disaster response workflow, a border surveillance workflow. Each new piece of data automatically triggers analyses and status updates. This structure increases perceived value and locks the customer into a repeatable operational cycle with Involve's system. Template libraries are reusable across customers in the same vertical, reducing delivery cost per new client.

Business Model Mechanics

The revenue-scaling flywheel

Five self-reinforcing loops — each cycle amplifies Involve's value proposition and competitive position

Involve's business model forms a classic scaling flywheel. The five loops are mutually reinforcing: each one generates an input to the next, and the cycle accelerates as each component matures. This is organic growth logic — no single event triggers it, but every successful mission and contract execution adds momentum.

FIG 2 — Revenue-scaling flywheel. Each loop amplifies the next. The cycle does not require an external trigger — every successful mission and contract renewal adds momentum to all five loops simultaneously.
Loop 1Fleet → Data
Each new balloon/sensor adds coverage days and area monitored, generating incremental proprietary data. The data accumulates as a time-series archive with compounding historical depth.
Loop 2Data → Intelligence
The expanded dataset improves CLEAR's analytics — deeper time series, more training examples, better anomaly baselines. Intelligence outputs become more accurate and comprehensive with every new data point.
Loop 3Intelligence → Contracts
Superior insights win more clients and larger deals. Existing customers expand scope — more sites, higher-tier CLEAR services — as trust grows. New verticals open with proven analytics that require no per-vertical R&D from scratch.
Loop 4Contracts → ARR
Larger, recurring monitoring contracts and CLEAR subscriptions increase Annual Recurring Revenue. A portion of ARR is reinvested into R&D and fleet expansion, closing the loop.
Loop 5ARR → Fleet
Reinvestment funds additional balloon platforms, sensor upgrades, and CLEAR platform development. This directly feeds Loop 1 — circling back to fleet growth and the next cycle of data accumulation.
Long-Range Commercial Vision

Multidomain commercial expansion

From stratospheric-only sensing today to a full multi-domain intelligence platform — a structural vision framed without overcommitment

Involve's long-term commercial plan envisions an evolution from stratospheric-only sensing to a truly multi-domain platform. This is not an immediate roadmap but a structural vision: each domain integration widens the intelligence offering and deepens customer lock-in. It parallels industry trends — Planet Labs + Quantum Systems' satellite–drone integration demonstrates that combining data across orbital and tactical domains delivers faster decision cycles and more resilient awareness than any single domain alone.

Current — Core
Stratospheric sensing
Using Stratostats® and Stratorelay® for broad-area monitoring. Persistence and low cost per km² — far cheaper than satellites for continuous coverage. Best suited to permissive airspaces with moderate-to-high resolution sensing requirements. The active revenue-generating foundation.
Near-term — Layer 2
Satellite augmentation
Integrating satellite imagery (optical, SAR) for worldwide reach — denied areas, open ocean, polar regions. Involve does not need to operate satellites: acquiring data through commercial providers or partnerships provides global context that complements stratospheric persistence. Together, the combination covers both the "always-on" theatre-level watch and the "everywhere" global snapshot.
Medium-term — Layer 3
Aerial & ground sensor integration
UAV/drone feeds (as Planet/Quantum are demonstrating) and ground IoT (thermal sensors on pipelines, ground cameras, industrial monitoring points) yield hyper-local data that stratospheric platforms cannot achieve at cost. HAPS acts as the strategic eye; UAV acts as the tactical investigator dispatched when HAPS detects an anomaly.
Long-term — Layer 4
Full intelligence orchestration
All domains feed into CLEAR, which prioritises and synthesises across them. A complete systems-of-systems view: space images detect a new structure (strategic alarm) → HAPS collects closer views → drone performs detailed inspection → ground sensors monitor environmental impact → CLEAR delivers the unified intelligence picture. This is the mature infrastructure model.
Organisation

Organisational scaling requirements

Six functions that must scale in parallel with the commercial strategy to sustain simultaneous civil and defence engagements

Function 01
Civil BD & Defence BD
Dedicated business development teams for each track. Civil BD focuses on industry verticals, commercial partners, and integrators — understands ROI and operational KPIs. Defence BD navigates procurement rules, security requirements, ministries, and EU/NATO frameworks. Domain expertise is not optional in either track.
Function 02
Enterprise Sales & Solutions Architecture
Senior enterprise sales force managing large accounts, upselling monitoring contracts, and closing CLEAR subscriptions. Solutions architects configure CLEAR proofs-of-concept and pilots — converting technical demonstrations into contracts. They translate customer operational workflows into CLEAR mission templates.
Function 03
Technical Pre-Sales & Integration
Strong technical pre-sales with geospatial and AI expertise. Works with customers to tailor workflows and ensure seamless integration of CLEAR with client IT systems — SCADA, GIS, ERPs. Also a feedback loop: pre-sales experience feeds directly into CLEAR product development prioritisation.
Function 04
Compliance & Security
Critical for defence and government contracts. Requires ISO 27001, GDPR, export control compliance, and potentially a "Trusted Supply Chain" certification for EU defence framework eligibility. A dedicated security and compliance function is a prerequisite for any material defence contract, not a nice-to-have.
Function 05
Operations & Fleet Delivery
Manages the stratospheric fleet, data pipelines, and platform uptime. As monitoring contracts grow, standardised SOPs for launch scheduling, data processing, and quality control ensure consistent, reliable delivery at the cadence contracts require. Operations is the delivery backbone that commercial commitments depend on.
Function 06
Customer Success & Renewals
High-touch account management to handle ongoing support, coordinate CLEAR co-development, and drive contract renewals. Gathers systematic feedback that feeds product roadmap. Directly responsible for net revenue retention — the metric that determines whether CLEAR's high gross margins translate into durable ARR growth.
Strategic Conclusions

Five strategic implications of the GTM architecture

How the commercial model underpins Involve's infrastructure thesis and Series A investment case

Infrastructure-grade asset construction
By tying together hardware, data networks, and analytics into a unified platform, Involve moves beyond a project shop to a backbone provider. The focus on long-term contracts and recurring SaaS mirrors how infrastructure is monetised in telecoms and cloud. Each new sensor or data feed is a "fibre strand" in Involve's network — adding value to every existing customer without incremental cost.
Dual-use hedges revenue risk
Serving both civil and defence markets hedges against cycle risk. If commercial projects stall in a downturn, government budgets and emergency response programmes maintain revenue. If defence budgets fluctuate, commercial SaaS subscriptions stabilise income. Investors prize this dual-stream because it broadens addressable market and smooths revenue cycles — a structural advantage over pure-commercial or pure-defence EO players.
Data ownership drives defensibility
Owning unique, continuous data cuts off competitors. Involve's proprietary stratospheric archive creates a high entry barrier — competitors cannot purchase historical stratospheric time-series from any other source. Customers rely on Involve for historical trend comparison and anomaly baselines (an effective switching cost at scale). This ownership is not replicable by acquiring analytics-only firms or purchasing satellite data.
Multi-domain leverage compounds the moat
Integrating across sensing domains gives structural leverage. As demonstrated by Planet/Quantum's satellite–drone integration, fusing data across orbital and tactical domains yields richer intelligence than either alone. Involve's long-term vision to orchestrate multi-layered sensing means CLEAR becomes the go-to geospatial decision-support platform for Europe and allied markets — analogous to Palantir's role in data fusion but with direct control of the underlying data source.
Series A investment has a coherent commercial home
Every element of the GTM — business model, sales approach, data roadmap, CLEAR development, organisational scaling — is internally consistent and mutually reinforcing. The Series A investment (fleet expansion, platform development, team growth) is not speculative; it directly unlocks the monitoring contract and CLEAR SaaS revenue tiers that the model depends on. The differentiated dual-track GTM (distinct civil vs defence paths, unified by CLEAR) ensures commercial focus while CLEAR ties all revenue streams into a single compounding growth engine.
Investor-grade synthesis: Involve's GTM strategy is analytical and systematic — it recognises real EO adoption dynamics (the "pilot trap," long defence cycles, the shift from price-per-km to insights-as-a-service) and structures each commercial path accordingly. By layering revenue streams, deepening customer engagement through data ownership, and scaling infrastructure logic, Involve builds a self-reinforcing commercial architecture that supports the vision of a continent-scale stratospheric intelligence platform without resorting to hyperbole.
