Executive Defence Thesis

Earth Intelligence infrastructure is inherently dual-use

The same persistent sensing, secure data processing, and decision support capabilities serve both civil and public-sector missions — this is physics, not positioning

Earth Intelligence sits at the convergence of persistent sensing, secure data processing, and decision support. These capabilities are intrinsically dual-use because the same physical phenomena and operational needs exist across civil and public-sector missions: border integrity, maritime safety, disaster response, environmental monitoring, and critical infrastructure protection all rely on timely geospatial situational awareness.

Involve is not a weapons manufacturer. We deliver stratospheric sensing infrastructure and Earth intelligence services: data collection at altitude, secure transmission, processing, and analytics outputs configurable for civil and government users. Our dual-use posture is an infrastructure posture — we build a persistent sensing layer and a secure analytics layer, and we integrate into institutional workflows where appropriate, without becoming a weapons prime.

Infrastructure, not weapons: In our product structure, we explicitly position ourselves as service-centric — "we do not sell hardware or software; instead, we deliver high-value services and data" — complemented by custom missions for clients with specific operational requirements, including defence and security users. This distinction is central to our procurement strategy, regulatory posture, and investor narrative.

Why Stratospheric Platforms Represent Strategic Sensing Infrastructure

Our platforms operate in the stratosphere at typically 20–30 km altitude, engineered for persistent monitoring and rapid tasking. The stratosphere provides a distinct operational sweet spot between drones and satellites: close enough to deliver very high-resolution imagery and low latency, yet high enough to enable wide-area coverage and persistence above most weather and conventional air traffic.

Dual-Use Framework

Civil and defence applications share the same stack

Dual-use is not a marketing label — it is the natural operating mode of Earth Intelligence infrastructure

Our technical stack is inherently dual-use because the platform physics, payload class, and data pipeline are identical across missions. The stratospheric persistence, high revisit, and wide-area coverage characteristics are the same whether the customer is an energy utility, a coast guard, or a national intelligence directorate. Only the assurance level, access controls, and retention policies differ — not the underlying infrastructure.

Infrastructure-grade monitoring for asset-intensive industries
Infrastructure monitoring, environmental assessment, precision agriculture, rapid mapping campaigns, and smart city operations. These missions generate high operational cadence, commercial datasets that scale CLEAR's training corpus, and compliance evidence that de-risks government procurement pathways.
Flywheel role: civil missions build operational maturity, telemetry, and dataset depth that improve performance on higher-assurance deployments

Sovereign advantage: European company · in-country platforms · data custody · no foreign IP dependency
Risk Mitigation Benefits of the Dual-Use Model
BENEFIT 01
Diversified demand across procurement cycles
Civil and governmental procurement cycles are not correlated. If defence investment is rising but specific programmes are delayed, commercial contracts maintain ARR. Vice versa, commercial downturns are hedged by government budget stability. European defence spending is structurally increasing but remains cyclical at programme level — dual-use reduces concentration risk on any single budget line.
BENEFIT 02
Platform utilisation and operational learning curves
Persistent civil missions generate operational telemetry, dataset growth, and reliability improvements that improve readiness for higher-assurance deployments — including government missions. Each commercial flight hour adds to the operational baseline that defence customers are buying when they select Involve as a strategic supplier.
BENEFIT 03
Compliance maturity through civil-scale operations
Operating at scale in civil contexts forces robust aviation safety, data protection, and cybersecurity controls — which are prerequisites for government procurement pathways. Civil compliance is not a diluted version of defence compliance; it is the foundational layer that qualifies Involve for the next tier of assurance requirements without starting from scratch.
BENEFIT 04
End-to-end capability aligned to institutional expectations
Institutional actors — EU DGs, MoDs, NATO command structures — increasingly expect end-to-end capability with "space-enabled services" and secure dissemination. Involve's full-stack model (platform → data → CLEAR analytics) matches this requirement. Pure data vendors cannot deliver the analytics layer; pure analytics vendors cannot guarantee the data pipeline.

Defence Use Cases

Seven mission domains — sensing and decision infrastructure, not weapon effects

In every case, Involve's role is to provide the persistent collection, processing, and analytics layer — not kinetic or weapon system integration

ISR & Persistent Surveillance
Core domain
Persistent ISR is a coverage problem, not a single-image problem. Decision makers need continuity, low latency, and the ability to retask rapidly when the situation evolves. Our Stratostats® architecture is explicitly designed for continuous, real-time data streams with high revisit at 20–30 km altitude, supported by autonomous wind-layer navigation via Neurostar®.

NATO budgets and doctrine fund persistent ISR forces explicitly and emphasise a permanent joint ISR system combining NATO and national assets — exactly the gap that stratospheric persistence fills between satellite revisit schedules and drone endurance limits.
Why stratosphere: Reduces observation gaps that satellite revisit cycles and drone endurance limits cannot close. Retaskable locally with low latency — continuous coverage over a defined theatre of interest.
Constraints: Airspace authorisation and coordination · payload power and bandwidth limits · weather and seasonal wind patterns · secure command, control, and dissemination requirements
Border Monitoring
Active pipeline
Border monitoring requires persistent detection of changes and events, not only periodic mapping. Operational value is highest in remote areas lacking terrestrial sensors, areas requiring repeatable monitoring, and national contexts where sovereignty and secure dissemination matter most.

Our pipeline includes border monitoring discussions and projects, including engagement with the Italian Air Force for national border monitoring applications. Continuous observation enables detection of patterns and anomalies over time — repeated routes, activity signatures — which sporadic satellite revisit cannot achieve.
Why stratosphere: Satellites provide wide coverage but weak continuous local persistence; drones provide local inspection but limited multi-day broad-area persistence and are subject to endurance constraints.
Constraints: Legal boundaries and privacy constraints in cross-border contexts · GDPR-aligned handling where imagery could include identifiable individuals · controlled access and data governance requirements
Maritime Domain Awareness
Active pipeline
Maritime awareness is a wide-area coverage challenge with a mix of strategic and operational needs: detection, classification, and monitoring of activity over large sea regions. Our commercial and government pipeline includes Mediterranean surveillance and maritime monitoring projects.

Persistence increases probability of detection of time-sensitive events and supports pattern-of-life analysis over a maritime area — complementing satellites and surface sensors. A single platform at 30 km can provide line-of-sight horizon coverage of ~619 km over open water without the endurance and basing limitations of tactical drones.
Why stratosphere: Satellites provide periodic SAR/optical coverage with cloud and revisit constraints; drones provide strong local inspection but limited continuous multi-hundred-kilometre coverage.
Constraints: Launch and recovery logistics in maritime environments · jurisdictional airspace requirements · communications availability for sustained data backhaul over open ocean
Critical Infrastructure Protection
Commercial + defence
Critical infrastructure protection is a monitoring and change detection problem: operators need early warning of anomalies, degradation, encroachments, and incidents. Our existing and pipeline activity includes infrastructure monitoring for pipelines, airports, roads, and critical infrastructure diligence with large industrial actors.

Persistent imaging supports higher-frequency change detection than typical satellite scheduling and can reduce the latency between event occurrence and detection — enabling proactive intervention rather than post-incident response.
Why stratosphere: Mid-layer advantage — higher persistence and coverage density than drones; lower latency and higher operational flexibility than satellites for a defined area of interest.
Constraints: Sensitive commercial data and national security-relevant locations require data segregation, encryption, and controlled access — particularly for government-adjacent facilities
Tactical Augmentation of Satellite ISR
CLEAR integration
In modern ISR architectures, the problem is not "satellites or aircraft" but multi-layer integration. NATO's Joint ISR model explicitly incorporates diverse collection assets across domains and highlights the need for fusion and dissemination. Our CLEAR roadmap is designed to integrate stratospheric imagery with satellite imagery (Copernicus and commercial), creating a fused knowledge base for analytics and reporting.

Stratospheric assets fill temporal gaps, provide local persistence during a crisis window, and support rapid retasking over a defined theatre — while satellites provide the baseline wide-area context that no single stratospheric platform can replicate.
Why stratosphere: Fills temporal coverage gaps between satellite passes; enables rapid retasking over a defined theatre without waiting for the next orbital window.
Constraints: Consistent georeferencing, processing pipeline governance, and licensing/security restrictions for sensitive or restricted EO datasets from third-party sources
Communications Relay, Disaster Response & Rapid Monitoring
Adjacent capability
High-altitude platforms are widely recognised as potential relay and resilient communications layers. The EU's GOVSATCOM and IRIS² programmes are explicitly designed to serve authorised users and security-critical missions. We support custom missions including telecom coverage in remote areas and private, secure communications solutions for government and corporate clients, subject to regulatory compliance and mission approvals.

In disaster response, continuity of observation and communications in the first hours and days is often the binding constraint. Persistent platforms reduce reliance on repeated sorties or unpredictable satellite tasking windows.
Why persistence matters: Disaster response requires continuous coverage during the critical first 72-hour window — persistent platforms eliminate the gaps that scheduled satellite passes and limited drone endurance create.
Constraints: Relay missions require spectrum coordination and regulatory approvals; disaster deployments require expedited but compliant aviation coordination and GDPR-aligned data governance
Strategic Monitoring: Supply Chain Resilience & Geopolitical Risk Detection
CLEAR-native
We treat strategic monitoring as a long-term downstream application of persistent EO and analytics. CLEAR is positioned to convert centimetre-level imagery into query-responsive intelligence, including automated reporting and multi-source integration for logistics nodes, energy corridors, and supply chain chokepoints. These use cases depend on robust governance and explainable analytics, because outputs may drive high-stakes decisions.
Heightened scrutiny: Strategic monitoring raises ethical questions and requires strong internal controls — appropriate use guarantees, lawful processing, access audit trails, and avoidance of misuse. These controls are engineered into CLEAR's access governance model, not added as afterthoughts.

EU DUAL-USE EXPORT CONTROL
Regulation (EU) 2021/821 — four practical implications
Export control assessment is not optional at scale and not limited to hardware. It can apply to:
  • Payload components meeting Annex I thresholds
  • Encryption or high-performance processing components
  • Technical assistance or software/technology transfer in cross-border contexts — including electronic transmission
  • Cyber-surveillance items not listed in Annex I, requiring due diligence and notification

We do not claim blanket non-control status — control thresholds depend on technical parameters of sensors, communications equipment, encryption, and end-use/end-user context.
AIRSPACE & AVIATION REGULATION
Higher airspace operations — developing EU regulatory area
EASA explicitly recognises higher airspace operations (20–40 km) as an emerging domain with no EU-level regulation yet in place. Some Member States have developed national regulations. ICAO Annex 2 includes operator obligations for heavy unmanned free balloons, including reporting requirements before planned descent.

We have embedded operational competence in authorisation workflows and maintain active monitoring of regulatory developments. This is a core operational competence, not an ancillary function — persistent stratospheric services cannot scale without repeatable regulatory pathways.
GDPR & DATA PROTECTION
Personal data implications in urban, border, and disaster contexts
Even where sensing is infrastructure-focused, urban monitoring, border contexts, and disaster response over populated areas can implicate personal data if individuals are identifiable or combined datasets increase identifiability. Our baseline approach:
  • Data minimisation aligned to mission objectives
  • Defined retention schedules and deletion policies
  • Role-based access, audit trails, controlled dissemination
  • Government-grade segregation and sovereign hosting on demand
SECURITY CLEARANCE PATHWAYS
Building a clearance-ready operating model for EUCI-adjacent programmes
Access to EU classified information is governed through Commission Decision (EU, Euratom) 2015/444 on protecting EUCI. For national MoD programmes, practical pathways involve personnel security clearances for specific staff, facility and information security controls aligned to national frameworks, and contractual compliance evidence.

We do not claim classified operations. Our objective is to build a clearance-ready operating model so that we can participate in programmes where security requirements expand over time — not to retroactively qualify after a contract is awarded.
Defence procurement requirement: MoDs and security agencies commonly require evidence of security management and information assurance, supply chain integrity and continuity planning, controlled access processes, and contractual ability to operate within specific sovereign constraints. We approach this as a long-cycle enterprise procurement motion rather than a transactional sales motion — consistent with our transition from pilots to framework agreements.
Security & Data Sovereignty

Security model — commercial workloads and government workloads by design

Segregation at the infrastructure layer, not only the application layer — with a controlled escalation path in assurance levels

PRINCIPLE 01
Segregation by design
Commercial workloads and datasets are separated from government workloads and datasets at the infrastructure and access layers, not only at the application layer. Government deployments have isolated storage, separate encryption key chains, and independent audit trails from commercial customer data.
PRINCIPLE 02
Least privilege and auditability
Government deployments require institutional-grade audit trails and strict access controls. NATO doctrine explicitly emphasises information assurance in the context of intelligence sharing. RBAC, multi-factor authentication, compartmentalisation, and immutable audit logs are build-to-spec controls, not post-hoc add-ons.
PRINCIPLE 03
Sovereignty options, not a fixed hosting choice
The EU Space Strategy for Security and Defence emphasises tailored delivery rules for security-sensitive services, including access control and controlled dissemination. Our architecture mirrors this: CLEAR is deployable in multiple sovereignty contexts — from commercial AWS/EU-region hosting to sovereign cloud providers to on-premise dedicated environments for sensitive deployments.
CLEAR Architecture — Security and Sovereignty Implications

CLEAR is defined internally as "Cloud-based LLM-enhanced Earth Analytics & Reconnaissance" — a cloud-native workflow converting centimetre-level imagery into query-responsive intelligence with minimal latency. Key architectural implications for government deployments:

Multi-source ingestion
Integration of Involve's stratospheric imagery plus satellite imagery (Copernicus and commercial) and potentially other sensor feeds. Government deployments can be restricted to specific source combinations — for example, Copernicus restricted data workflows for accredited users, without exposure to commercial data pipelines.
Automated analytics
Object detection, super-resolution, change detection, and report generation. Government-grade deployments include explainability requirements — analytics outputs must trace to source data and processing parameters to support decision accountability in high-stakes contexts.
Interface layer
Natural language queries yielding answers with supporting maps and explanations. For government users, the interface layer can be deployed in isolated network environments — on-premise or sovereign-cloud — without dependence on public internet infrastructure for query processing or model inference.
Current statusTRL 5 → 7
We treat CLEAR's current TRL 5 status and target TRL 7 as a roadmap commitment rather than a finished capability claim. The TRL 7 target is aligned to our EIC workplan, which includes dedicated work packages on data commercialisation and security/compliance investments for the cloud platform. Current AWS hosting is treated as a procurement variable, not a fixed technical decision — sovereign cloud migration is scoped as a mid-term capability.
EU hosting strategy: We support EU-based hosting and sovereign options through EU-region hosting with customer-controlled key management, sovereign cloud providers and national frameworks where required, and on-premise or dedicated environments for sensitive deployments. This is consistent with EU institutional practice for restricting access to security-sensitive Copernicus data through segregated workflows and accredited user registries.

Risk Register

Risk factors and mitigations

Risk disclosure is a core element of institutional readiness — dual-use infrastructure attracts scrutiny and must be managed with clear governance

Regulatory risk Medium
Airspace regimes for higher-altitude operations are evolving. EASA explicitly recognises higher airspace operations as an emerging domain with no single EU-level regulation today, creating multi-jurisdictional complexity. Regulatory frameworks can shift, tighten, or create inconsistencies across Member States that affect mission authorisation timelines.
Mitigation: Embedded operational competence in authorisation workflows; active monitoring of regulatory developments as a core operational function, not an ancillary activity; progressive scaling with built-in authorisation lead times per jurisdiction
Political & procurement risk Medium
Defence and security procurement is exposed to budget reprioritisation, programme delays, and changing institutional requirements — even in a structurally growing spending environment. EU defence investment is rising, but procurement cycles remain long and sensitive to political shifts. Individual programmes can be delayed by 12–36+ months without advance notice.
Mitigation: Dual-use diversification reduces dependence on single programmes; phased procurement strategy with civil revenue providing baseline ARR during defence cycle delays; multiple national engagements reduce single-country concentration
Export control & cross-border delivery risk Managed
Regulation (EU) 2021/821 can apply to hardware, software, technology transfer, and technical assistance — including electronic transmission. As Involve scales cross-border, the compliance surface area expands: payload components, encryption modules, technical training, and even software demonstrations can trigger licensing requirements in some jurisdictions.
Mitigation: Structured export control governance, per-component classification playbooks, end-user screening procedures, licensing where required; export control assessment embedded into product development and commercial processes from the outset
Airspace constraints & operational risk Low-Medium
Persistent deployments require repeatable authorisation approvals, safe ascent/descent procedures, and coordination obligations for unmanned free balloons — including descent reporting obligations under ICAO Annex 2. Obtaining authorisations in new jurisdictions can take 3–12 months and is not guaranteed in advance.
Mitigation: Conservative flight safety engineering; mission SOPs with redundancy planning; progressive geographic scaling that allows authorisation timelines to be built into business development planning; existing authorisation relationships in Italy as the scaling template
Ethical scrutiny of dual-use technologies Medium
Persistent sensing capabilities can raise ethical questions regarding surveillance, misuse, and proportionality — amplified by AI-driven analytics and automated reporting features. As CLEAR's capabilities grow, so does the potential for misuse or reputational challenge from specific customer or use-case choices.
Mitigation: Use-case review processes; contractual constraints on application scope; governance controls (access control, audit logging, data retention limits); remaining within lawful and regulated end-use boundaries; explicit internal policy on prohibited use cases
CLEAR maturity risk Low-Medium
CLEAR is at TRL 5 today with a target of TRL 7 under the EIC-aligned workplan. Government procurement expectations for analytics platforms can exceed the current capability state, creating a gap between what can be contracted and what can be delivered on the timelines defence customers expect.
Mitigation: Transparent TRL framing in all government discussions; staged capability claims that match the development roadmap; Series A funding directly targets CLEAR platform development and security compliance investment to close the gap ahead of major B2G procurement timelines
Long-Term Positioning

Four structural drivers of long-term defensibility

European Earth Intelligence infrastructure — positioned at the intersection of expanding security budgets, persistent ISR demand, dual-use resilience, and infrastructure-grade defensibility

European security budgets are structurally expanding
EU Member States' defence expenditure reached €343bn in 2024 (estimated €381bn in 2025). Defence investment reached €106bn in 2024 — up 42% vs 2023. The EDF (€7.953bn), EU Space Programme (€14.88bn), and IRIS² (€2.4bn) are multi-year committed funding instruments, not discretionary budgets. This is not a cycle; it is a structural reset of European security spending that will sustain procurement demand for persistent sensing infrastructure for at least the 2021–2030 planning horizon.
Doctrine demands persistent, multi-domain ISR
NATO's Joint ISR framework emphasises interoperability and the networking environment for secure ISR dissemination. NATO common-funded budget ceilings explicitly fund ISR forces (€264m NISRF, €420m AWACS for 2024). Institutional doctrine increasingly treats persistent multi-domain sensing as a baseline requirement, not an optional capability uplift — positioning Involve's stratospheric layer as a necessary complement to satellite and drone ISR, not a discretionary purchase.
Dual-use is a resilience mechanism for the company
Civil markets expand Involve's TAM, increase platform utilisation and dataset generation, and support a sustainable transition from pilots to recurring contracts. Commercial revenue provides the baseline ARR stability that allows defence procurement cycles (12–36+ months) to be managed without existential cash flow risk. The dual-use model is how infrastructure companies survive the long procurement timelines that would otherwise make defence-only models extremely capital-intensive.
Infrastructure positioning is strategically defensible
Infrastructure positioning supports partnerships with primes without creating competitive conflict; complements satellites without requiring orbital capital expenditure; and avoids the regulatory, ethical, and reputational pitfalls associated with developing weapon systems. This positioning is consistent with our Series A narrative and EIC-aligned roadmap: scalable stratospheric platforms plus a CLEAR analytics layer that converts data into decision outputs, with sovereign deployment options and institutional-grade governance — a European Earth Intelligence infrastructure provider, not a defence contractor.
Series A investor framing: The defence and dual-use thesis is not a speculative overlay on a commercial business — it is the second revenue channel of a dual-track model that expands TAM, increases contract duration and ACV, and deepens switching costs through sovereign data custody. Every Series A-funded capability (fleet expansion, CLEAR platform maturation, compliance infrastructure) simultaneously serves civil and defence markets. The marginal cost of adding a second revenue channel to an existing infrastructure platform is the strongest possible argument for the investment.
