Strategic Overview

Three platforms, three parallel programmes

Each programme is at a different maturity and serves a distinct role in the scaling strategy

Involve Space operates three concurrent hardware development programmes that build on one another. Stratorelay® is already commercial — the workhorse for on-demand missions and the primary near-term revenue driver. Stratostats® v2 is the Series A execution programme — unlocking weeks-to-months-long persistent missions and the defense contract pipeline. LoonHive® is the long-range programme — turning individual platforms into a sensing constellation and unlocking continental-scale intelligence.

The three programmes are not sequential. They run in parallel, with Stratorelay® generating commercial revenue that partially funds the v2 and LoonHive® programmes. Each programme's TRL advancement directly de-risks the next funding round and unlocks a new tier of customer contracts.

Stratorelay® Programme

Stratorelay® — scaling the commercial engine

From proof-of-concept to operational tempo: the revenue foundation of the business

Stratorelay® reached TRL 9 and full commercial status in 2024. The programme is no longer in a development phase — the engineering challenges are solved. The roadmap is now about operational scaling: increasing mission cadence, expanding the operator network, building a multi-launch logistics capability, and hardening the Neurostar® deployment planning module to shorten the flight-readiness window.

The two strategic upgrades in the 2026–2027 window are: (1) a 24-hour rapid deployment protocol targeting defense customers who need persistent but time-critical area coverage, and (2) a multi-launch capability allowing simultaneous Stratorelay® deployments from different sites to cover overlapping or adjacent areas — the precursor to fleet thinking without the full LoonHive® coordination overhead.

Current status
Operational — 200+ flights, active contracts
Stratorelay® is flying commercially with paying customers across three verticals: Earth observation and mapping (demonstrated 1,285 km² coverage over Rome in a single mission), payload IOD rideshare (over 40 payload types elevated), and defense area surveillance (under the Italian Ministry of Defense contract). The platform requires no further TRL advancement — it is operationally mature.
2026–2027 focus
Mission tempo · 24h protocol · multi-site ops
The Series A investment enables three operational scaling actions: hiring dedicated mission operations personnel to run 30+ missions/year (vs ~8 in 2024), developing the 24h rapid-deployment package for defense customers, and acquiring additional ground equipment to enable simultaneous multi-site launches. None of these require new technology development — they are execution and logistics investments.

Stratostats® Programme

Stratostats® — from days to months of endurance

v1 is the proven foundation; v2 unlocks the persistent mission market

Stratostats® v1 reached TRL 9 with 30+ commercial flights and over 100 test flights. It can station-keep for multi-day missions. Stratostats® v2 is the programme that extends endurance from days to 90+ days — the critical threshold for persistent ISR, long-range environmental monitoring, and sustained defense operations.

The v2 programme has four parallel workstreams: ACS upgrade (new compressor with higher duty cycle), EPS upgrade (larger battery capacity and solar area), Neurostar® v2 (refined RL model with longer-range wind prediction), and balloon envelope redesign (reduced gas permeability for multi-month inflation retention). TRL 7 is the current state — ground testing complete, initial flight tests underway.

Stratostats® v1 validated the entire ACS concept, the gondola structure, the Neurostar® navigation core, and the ground operations protocol. It is the platform that flew the MoD contract entry missions and all commercial EO campaigns to date.

Four parallel upgrades to reach 90-day persistence

v2 is not a redesign of v1 — it is a targeted upgrade across four subsystems that each limit long-duration operations. The ACS compressor is redesigned for a higher duty cycle, allowing more frequent altitude corrections over longer missions without motor wear. The EPS increases total battery capacity to 1,800 Wh and adds panel area to improve the solar charge balance at higher latitudes and winter sun angles.

The balloon envelope switches to a lower gas permeability film to reduce helium/hydrogen leakage over months-long missions — the principal reason v1 is limited to days. The Neurostar® v2 navigation model is retrained with a longer-range wind forecast horizon (5–10 days vs 2–3 days in v1) to enable proactive trajectory planning for multi-week station-keeping.

LoonHive® Programme

LoonHive® — building the stratospheric constellation

From single-platform to coordinated fleet: the long-range value creation programme

LoonHive® is the programme that transforms Involve Space from a platform company into a persistent stratospheric sensing network operator. It is the architecture for coordinating multiple Stratostats® units as a single intelligent fleet — with Neurostar® managing the joint flight plan to maximize coverage of a defined area while maintaining separation constraints and individual platform integrity.

The programme has two phases: simulation-first (running hundreds of coordination scenarios computationally to validate the optimization algorithm before expensive multi-balloon test flights), and incremental flight validation (2-balloon → 5-balloon → 10+ balloon flight series, each expanding the validated operational envelope). The first dual-balloon coordinated flight is planned for 2026.

The coordination problem
Why classical solvers fail at fleet scale
Each balloon in the fleet must be assigned an altitude trajectory that (a) moves it toward useful coverage positions, (b) maintains safe separation from adjacent balloons, (c) stays within each balloon's individual ACS capability envelope, and (d) maximizes the joint coverage function. For N balloons across a 4 km altitude band divided into M discrete levels, the joint action space grows as M^N. At 10 balloons and 10 altitude levels, this is 10 billion combinations. Classical solvers explore this space exhaustively — taking 11 days for 150 balloons on modern hardware.
Neurostar® fleet optimizer
QUBO reformulation + quantum-inspired solver
The Neurostar® fleet module reformulates the coordination problem as a Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) — a form amenable to variational quantum and quantum-inspired solvers. Running on GPU-accelerated hardware with a simulated annealing + variational eigensolver approach, the same 150-balloon problem solves in minutes. This is not a marginal speedup — it is the difference between the fleet being operationally plannable and unmanageable. No other HAPS operator is known to have solved this at scale.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing readiness and supply chain

Scaling from prototype production to series manufacturing alongside the platform roadmap

The platform roadmap is tightly coupled to manufacturing readiness. Stratorelay® and Stratostats® v1 are produced in low-rate series production using a combination of in-house assembly and specialized external suppliers. The v2 programme and LoonHive® fleet scaling require a transition to a higher-volume production model — the Series A investment allocates resources specifically for this transition.

Risk Analysis

Technical and programme risks

Known risks across the three platform programmes with mitigation approaches

v2 envelope gas retention
Medium
The new low-permeability envelope film must retain gas for 90+ days in the stratospheric environment — UV radiation, extreme temperature cycling, and mechanical stress from altitude changes. Film performance in long-duration conditions is only partially validated at ground level.
Mitigation: Accelerated aging tests underway. Flight test programme designed to incrementally validate 7 → 30 → 90 day thresholds before committing to full commercial deployment.
ACS duty cycle at 90 days
Medium
The compressor must operate intermittently across a 90-day mission. Motor wear, bearing lubrication behaviour, and thermal cycling at stratospheric temperatures over this duration have not been fully characterized in flight.
Mitigation: NablaWave has extended the motor's operational specification based on modified lubrication. Redundant valve actuator ensures descent capability even if compressor fails. Flight validation is the definitive test.
Neurostar® wind forecast accuracy
Low–Medium
Station-keeping performance depends on the accuracy of wind layer forecasts from ECMWF and onboard sensors. Forecast errors accumulate over longer missions, and in anomalous meteorological conditions the RL agent may have insufficient information to maintain position.
Mitigation: Deterministic algorithm always runs in parallel as fallback. v2 extends the forecast horizon to 10 days and includes an uncertainty estimator that widens the station-keeping budget in low-confidence conditions.
LoonHive® first coordinated flight
Medium
The dual-balloon coordinated flight is the first time the LoonHive® coordination algorithm will be tested in real-world conditions. Simulation has validated the approach, but atmospheric variability and communication latency may produce unexpected behaviours.
Mitigation: Both balloons carry full independent safety systems. Ground override command available at all times. First dual-flight uses conservative separation margins 3× the operational minimum. Incremental parameter tightening across subsequent flights.
Regulatory airspace approval
Low
Multi-balloon operations may require new airspace coordination frameworks with national aviation authorities that are designed around single-vehicle NOTAM filings. Some jurisdictions may require special authorization for fleet operations.
Mitigation: Legal team engaged with ENAC (Italy) and Eurocontrol on fleet operations framework. First dual-flight planned for uncongested airspace. ESA and EDA relationships provide pathway for regulatory fast-tracking in NATO context.
Envelope supplier capacity
Low
Current envelope supplier can supply approximately 15 units per year. Fleet programme requires 30+ by 2027. Single-source dependency creates risk if supplier encounters production or quality issues.
Mitigation: Second supplier qualification already scoped in Series A plan. Existing supplier informed of volume growth trajectory. Inventory buffer planned for Q4 2026 build-up before fleet programme ramp.
Market Position

How the roadmap creates competitive distance

Each milestone widens the gap against HAPS competitors and satellite alternatives

The Stratostats® v2 and LoonHive® programmes are not just product upgrades — they are strategic moat-widening events. Each TRL step creates a new capability threshold that competitors must independently cross. The progression from single-platform to fleet is particularly hard to replicate: it requires not just more hardware, but a fleet coordination algorithm and a manufacturing scale-up that takes years to build and validate.
